Abstract

TimeMosaic introduces a novel forecasting framework that explicitly addresses temporal heterogeneity in multivariate time series. Existing patch-based methods rely on fixed-length segmentation, assuming uniform information density across time, which leads to detail loss in complex regions and redundancy in stable intervals. To overcome these limitations, TimeMosaic proposes two key components: Adaptive Patch Embedding (APE) and Segment-wise Decoding (SD). APE dynamically adjusts patch granularity based on local information density, balancing motif reuse and structural clarity while preserving temporal continuity. SD further models decoding heterogeneity by treating each prediction horizon as a distinct yet related subtask, implemented via prompt-based tuning, which allows horizon-specific adaptation without modifying shared encoder parameters.

Extensive experiments on 17 real-world datasets (covering domains such as electricity, weather, traffic, and wind) demonstrate that TimeMosaic achieves consistent state-of-the-art results in long-term forecasting. When trained on a large-scale corpus containing 321 billion observations, it attains performance comparable to leading time-series foundation models like Chronos and TimesFM, with far fewer parameters. Overall, TimeMosaic provides a unified, adaptive, and efficient solution for modeling heterogeneous temporal dynamics.
