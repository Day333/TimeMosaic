Introduction

TimeMosaic is a novel time-series forecasting framework that explicitly models temporal heterogeneity—the non-uniform information density across time. Traditional patch-based forecasting models divide sequences into fixed-length patches, implicitly assuming that all temporal regions carry equal complexity. This assumption leads to redundant encoding in stable regions and detail loss in volatile ones.

To address this limitation, TimeMosaic introduces the first dynamic patch segmentation mechanism in the field of time-series forecasting. The proposed Adaptive Patch Embedding (APE) adjusts patch granularity according to local temporal complexity, achieving a balance between representation efficiency and information fidelity. Meanwhile, the Segment-wise Decoding (SD) module models horizon-specific heterogeneity through prompt-based tuning, allowing each prediction step to adapt independently while maintaining global coherence.

Experiments on 17 real-world datasets covering electricity, weather, traffic, and wind domains show that TimeMosaic achieves consistent state-of-the-art results. When trained on a large-scale corpus with 321 billion observations, it reaches performance comparable to foundation models like Chronos and TimesFM, but with significantly fewer parameters.
